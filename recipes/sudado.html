Sudado de Pollo

Description

Sudado de Pollo is a typical Colombian dish of chicken, potato and other starches (eg yuca, plantain) in a light savory guiso of spices, onion, and tomato.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • 4 Potatoes
  • 1 Yuca
  • 2 Tomatoes
  • 1 Onion
  • 1/2 Bell Pepper
  • Oil
  • Chicken seasonings
    • Garlic and Salt powder
    • Adobo Goya
    • Cumin
  • Half Glass Water
  • 1 Adobo Goya con Azafran packet
  • Cilantro

Steps

  1. Preheat oil in a pan on low medium heat.
  2. Wash and cut the chicken.
  3. Season the chicken with the Chicken seasonings.
  4. Place chicken on the hot oil, set heat to medium, and sear on both sides.
  5. While chicken is searing, dice tomatoes, onion, and bell pepper.
  6. Once chicken is seared, add diced onions and bell peppers and let them sear.
  7. While onion and bell peppers are searing, cut potatoes into thick slices and yuca into medium chunks.
  8. Once onion and bell peppers are seared, add the potatoes, yuca, tomatoes, and water. Set heat to medium high and bring to a boil.
  9. Once it's boiling, add Adobo Goya con Azafran packet and mix well. Set heat to medium and cover.
  10. Let simmer for 5-10 more minutes until potatoes and yucas are cooked through.
  11. While you wait, chop the cilantro.
  12. Once ready, garnish with cilantro and enjoy with rice.
